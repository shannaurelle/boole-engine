Expressions

An expression for simplification have the following rules

  1. Variables are named after letters in the English alphabet (e.g. a,b,c)
  2. Terms are made by connecting one or more different variables together, or it might just be 1 or 0 only (e.g. 0,1,a,bc,def)
  3. Expressions are made by one or more terms together with a plus sign separating them (e.g. a+b, ab+c,def+bc+gh)
  4. Expressions can have expressions inside of them enclosed in parentheses with a term or expression before the open parenthesis or after the closing parenthesis (e.g. a+b(c+d) , (b+c)s , (a + (b+c)(d+e)) )

An important consequence of a simplified expression is that this expression is stated in algebraic normal form.
